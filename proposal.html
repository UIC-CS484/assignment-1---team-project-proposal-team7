HOTSPOT

RESTful API

  • We are planning to use Google Maps REST APIs. It will give users the ability to rate a place and save/bookmark it in their profile.
  • The places they rate will show up on their map and to the public only if they choose to. Places with more ratings will show up "hotter" on the map, whereas places that are not as popular will have a cooler color indicating a lower popularity.
  • Authenticated users will be asked to sign up only if they try to rate a place, otherwise users can view the "hot" places in town without signing up. However, to rate a place, a user will have to sign up using either phone or email and set up a password. We won't ask the users for any more information.
  • Users will be asked to complete their profile by setting a username if they want to go public with their liked places.
  • We will also store the data of restaurants/shops that users like.

Proposed database entities:

  • USER - user_id, fname, lname, email, phone, password, friends_list, created_at, updated_at
  • LOCATION - location_id, name, google_metadata, liked_by_count, created_at, updated_at
  • LOC_USER_REL- id, user_id, location_id

User type and Goal

People who want to travel to new places and check out what's good beforehand. Also people who are interested in other people's suggestions, or suggestions from those out of their immediate circle.

Goals: - Provide an easy way to recommend shop/restaurants etc. - Use intuitive visual cues like a heat map on google maps to show places that are "hot" and that are not. - User can choose to share their suggestions with people close them (location wise)
